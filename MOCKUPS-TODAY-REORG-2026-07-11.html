The diagnosis in one line: ten strata, eight data sources, and one urgent commitment can legitimately render in up to six of them (outlook, this-week, coming-up, focus, stalled, needs-attention). The crowding isn't card styling — it's that sections are organized by which engine surface produced them, not by what the user is trying to do. Also: two full LLM narratives of the same corpus stack at the top whenever the viewer has an identity.

Pro: this IS the product thesis — two workers, one field, check-ins as reconciliation; incoming triage gets the standing first slot §C2 demands; the packet also carries the doctrine's per-moment framing, so morning Today (plan + triage) can legitimately differ from evening Today (results + tomorrow's staging) with zero extra plumbing. Con: only real when a day-plan capture exists (live on Ross's corpus since 2026-07-11, not yet in Trisha's workflow) — the no-plan fallback is load-bearing, and shipping the fallback IS Concept B.

Concept B · "Three questions, one appearance per item" — no new plumbing; same eight sources, reorganized by user intent into three bands: Needs you (act) · Next two weeks (watch) · Where things stand (read). Hard rule: a record renders ONCE, in the band of its strongest signal, with quiet chips instead of re-renders. Kills the double SoP (one narrative, lens toggle), merges outlook + this-week + coming-up into one runway list, folds Focus/Stalled into sort order + chips on that list, demotes the needs-attention rollup into the read band.
